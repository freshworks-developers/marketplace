# what are the steps to publish a Global app as a Freshworks App

## Summary
1. Log in and Navigate  
2. App Details  
3. Pricing and Details  
4. Submit and Review  
5. Post‑Approval (Published)  
6. Managing Existing Apps  

## Step 1: Log in and Navigate
1. Log in to your Freshworks account.  
2. From the left navigation pane, go to the apps gallery.  
3. Click **Go to Developer Portal**.  
4. Click **New App** and select **Freshworks App**.

## Step 2: App Details
1. **Upload App File:** Click **Upload App file**, select your packed app file, and click **Open**.  
2. **App Type:** Choose **Freshworks App** (unless it’s a paid app).  
3. **Name Your App:** Enter a concise 2–3 word name.  
4. Click **Proceed** to populate the **Works with** section with supported modules.

## Step 3: Pricing and Details
1. **Paid App (Optional):** Select **This is a paid app** and enter pricing details.  
2. **App Information:** Complete fields in the **App information** tab.  
3. **Multi‑Language Support (Optional):** Add languages and fill in details for each.  
4. Click **Save** to persist your changes.

## Step 4: Submit and Review
1. Click **Save and Publish** to submit the app for approval.  
2. Use **View Activities** to track the review progress.

## Step 5: Post‑Approval (Published)
1. Once approved, the app appears as **Live Version (Published)** in the code file section.  
2. **Selective Access (Optional):** Hide the app from the marketplace and share a private URL.  
3. **View App Insights:** Monitor user ratings, reviews, and uninstall feedback.

## Step 6: Managing Existing Apps
1. **Edit or Delete:** Rejected apps can be edited or removed.  
2. **Upload New Version:** Add features or modules:
   1. **Auto Update (Optional):** Enable automatic updates for users.  
   2. **Release Notes (Mandatory):** Provide update details in all supported languages.  
3. Click **Save and Publish** to resubmit the new version for review.

## Notes:
- Paid apps must meet specific criteria and cannot be converted to free apps later.  
- New versions must support all previously supported modules.  
- Users will see update information and can choose when to update their apps.

# what are the steps to publish a Global app as a Custom App

## Summary
1. Log in and Navigate  
2. Create Custom App  
3. Select Custom App Type  
4. Upload Zip File  
5. Name Your App  
6. Proceed to Details  
7. Review Auto‑Populated Details  
8. Fill App Information  
9. Save and Test  
10. Install for Testing  
11. Uninstall Test  
12. Disable Test  
13. Functional Testing  
14. Submit for Publishing  
15. View Activities  
16. Add New Version  
17. Publish the App  

## Step 1: Log in and Navigate
1. Log in to your Freshworks product account.  
2. From the left navigation pane, go to the apps gallery.  
3. Click **Go to Developer Portal**.  
4. Click **New App** and select **Custom App**.

## Step 2: Select Custom App Type
1. On the **Create a new app** page, choose **Custom App** as the app type.

## Step 3: Upload Zip File
1. In the **Upload Zip file** section, click to select and upload your packed app file.  
2. To replace it later, click **Replace App file** and upload the new version.

## Step 4: Name Your App
1. Enter a meaningful 2–3 word name in the **Name your App** field.  
2. Click **Proceed**.

## Step 5: Review Auto‑Populated Details
The left details panel will show:
- App name and **Draft** status  
- Status indicators for Code Upload, Display Details, Support Details  
- App type, App Id, and Last updated timestamp  
- Code File section with **Draft Version** status

## Step 6: Fill App Information
1. In the main **App information** tab, complete all required fields.  
2. Click **Save and test** to validate before publishing.

## Step 7: Install for Testing
1. In the **Manage Apps > In Development** tab (opens in a new tab), click **Install** next to your app.  
2. If third‑party auth is required, enter credentials and click **Authorize**.  
3. If installation parameters are needed, fill in values and click **Install**.

## Step 8: Uninstall and Disable Tests
1. To test uninstallation: go to **Settings > Uninstall App**.  
2. To test disabling: toggle **Enable app** off under **Settings**.

## Step 9: Functional Testing
1. Verify the app’s behavior within the product UI.  
2. Ensure all features work as expected.

## Step 10: Submit for Publishing
1. Navigate back to the apps gallery and **Go to Developer Portal**.  
2. Under **All Apps**, find your custom app in development.  
3. Click its card to open the details page.

## Step 11: View Activities
1. In the left panel, click **View activities** to see all testing and submission logs.

## Step 12: Add New Version
1. Click **Add new version** to upload updates for your in‑development custom app.

## Step 13: Publish the App
1. Once ready, click **Publish** to make your custom app available to users.
